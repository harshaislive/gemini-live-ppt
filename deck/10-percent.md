# The 10% Life / 10% Lifestyle

## What it is

The **10% Lifestyle** is Beforest's membership model.

It is framed as:
- **30 person-nights per year**
- for **10 years**
- across Beforest collectives
- as **access, not ownership**

The high-level promise is:
- protect 10% of the year
- so the other 90% becomes more focused, rested, and human

## Core framing

This is not sold as:
- leisure
- tourism
- a resort package
- real estate
- a timeshare pitch

It is sold as:
- a recurring reset practice
- a rhythm of return
- intentional living over a decade
- access to restored land that can hold recovery

## Membership structure

### Main structure
- 30 person-nights per year
- 10-year term
- 300 total nights across the decade
- access across Beforest collectives

### Usage rules
- immediate family can participate
- children under 12 do not count toward person-nights
- nights do **not** carry forward
- 2:1 weekday-to-weekend balance is used to keep access workable
- subject to availability

### Why it is structured this way
The product logic is:
- rhythm matters more than accumulation
- people postpone ownership-style products
- access with built-in rhythm changes behavior faster
- the point is repeated return, not stockpiling unused entitlement

## Pricing framing rules

These are critical:

Never:
- reduce the offer to per-night math
- reduce it to per-day affordability logic
- use budget / value / cheap language
- present it like a travel discount

Always frame it as:
- 30 nights per year for 10 years
- 300 nights of intentional living
- a premium, long-horizon commitment
- a practice of protecting time

A comparison like **"less than a business-class flight"** may be used in source materials, but not cost-per-day or cost-per-night logic.

## What the 10% idea means

The 10% message is philosophical before it is numerical.

It means:
- you do not need to escape your whole life
- you need to protect a meaningful fraction of it
- regular recovery changes the rest of the year
- reset works when it is repeated

So the deck does not ask:
- "Can you take a long break someday?"

It asks:
- "Can you protect enough of the year to keep returning well?"

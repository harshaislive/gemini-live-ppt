# Flow — Beforest / The 10% Life

## 1) Core story arc

The canonical Beforest deck follows a **7-step emotional progression**:

1. **Tension**
   - "You've been running at 100%."
   - Name exhaustion and the quiet erosion of overcapacity.

2. **Reframe**
   - Protecting 10% of the year is enough to change the other 90%.
   - This is **not a holiday**. It is a **practice of return**.

3. **Proof**
   - Beforest earns trust through land, time, and evidence.
   - 7 years, 6 collectives, 1,300 acres, 250 families.

4. **Experience**
   - Make the life tangible.
   - Beforest is about belonging to restored places, not consuming amenities.

5. **Clarity**
   - Explain the structure plainly.
   - 30 person-nights per year for 10 years.
   - Access, not ownership.

6. **Urgency**
   - Not sales pressure.
   - The real cost is another year without protected reset.

7. **Action**
   - Experience before commitment.
   - Trial stay first.
   - "You decide with your feet, not your eyes."

### Canonical rules for the flow

- Each slide is a **scene**, not a data point.
- Narration carries meaning; visuals carry atmosphere.
- Keep on-screen text minimal.
- Speak to **one person**, never a room.
- Do not use vacation / getaway language.
- Do not use cost-per-night framing.
- The trial stay is the **pilot**, not the backup plan.

## 4) Slide-by-slide flow notes

### Slide 1 — Tension
**Title:** `Full calendars do not make full lives.`

Function:
- hold up the mirror
- name the quiet erosion behind ambition
- establish that restoration is maintenance, not luxury

Key idea:
- the problem is not output
- the problem is having no protected rhythm for recovery

### Slide 2 — Quote pause
**Title:** `You can get very good at a life that is quietly hurting you.`

Function:
- slow the presentation down
- let the emotional truth land before the solution appears

### Slide 3 — Reframe
**Title:** `You do not need escape. You need protected time.`

Function:
- introduce the 10% idea as **recurring protected time**
- reject the idea of dramatic escape
- connect 30 nights/year to repeated recovery and trust in place

Key idea:
- this is not a one-off break
- this is a rhythm of return

### Slide 4 — Proof
**Title:** `Trust should be built on land, not language.`

Function:
- earn trust with evidence
- move from claim to proof

Approved proof points:
- 7 years of work
- 6 collectives
- 1,300 acres restored
- 250 families already in the rhythm

### Slide 5 — Quote pause
**Title:** `Rest is not a reward. It is a rhythm worth protecting.`

Function:
- compress the philosophy into one sentence
- reset attention before the experiential section

### Slide 6 — Experience
**Title:** `Beforest is where recovery becomes real.`

Function:
- make Beforest feel like a lived system of return
- shift from concept to embodied life

Key idea:
- these are not amenities
- they are restored places where belonging becomes possible

### Slide 7 — Clarity
**Title:** `Access changes behaviour faster than ownership.`

Function:
- explain the model
- answer practical objections
- keep structure aligned with the brand

Core structure:
- 30 person-nights per year
- 10 years
- 300 nights total across the decade
- access, not ownership
- no carry-forward
- rhythm, not accumulation

### Slide 8 — Quote pause
**Title:** `You decide with your feet, not your eyes.`

Function:
- set up the CTA philosophy
- shift from explanation to action

### Slide 9 — Urgency
**Title:** `The cost of waiting is another year unchanged.`

Function:
- create urgency without sounding salesy
- frame delay as lost reset, lost calibration, lost presence

Key idea:
- every postponed year is another year left unprotected

### Slide 10 — Action / CTA
**Title:** `Start with the smallest real step.`

Function:
- move to trial stay
- reduce abstraction
- let the land become the final closer

CTA:
- `Start your trial`
- `https://hospitality.beforest.co`

CTA framing:
- Blyton Bungalow is the pilot
- experience before commitment
- every rupee of the trial stay adjusts toward membership when they join

## 5) Important structural nuance

There is a **message variant** across artifacts:

- some source files phrase the reframe as **"What if 90% was enough?"**
- the broader brand logic is still **protect 10% of the year**
- the current runtime deck expresses that same idea through lines like:
  - `You do not need escape. You need protected time.`
  - `30 person-nights per year for 10 years`
  - `The other 90% gets better`

So the deck is structurally consistent even where individual headlines vary.

---

## 6) One-line summary of the flow

**The deck starts by naming exhaustion, reframes rest as protected time, proves Beforest through land and stewardship, explains the 10% model, then closes by moving the person toward a trial stay as the smallest real step.**

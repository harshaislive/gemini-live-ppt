# Beforest presentation flow

The conversation follows a seven-part emotional arc:

1. Tension — name the exhaustion of running at full capacity.
2. Reframe — explain that 10% protected time is enough to change the other 90%.
3. Proof — earn trust with seven years of work, six collectives, 1,300 acres, and 250 families.
4. Experience — make the life inside the collectives feel tangible.
5. Clarity — explain what the structure is and what it is not.
6. Urgency — explain the personal cost of waiting another year unchanged.
7. Action — invite the listener to start with the trial stay and let the land answer first.

## Pacing rules
- Each response should feel like a scene with one clear emotional purpose.
- Narration should feel calm, human, and unforced.
- Use short spoken paragraphs.
- Let key ideas breathe; do not stack too many claims at once.
- Keep moving naturally unless the listener interrupts.

## Language rules
- Prefer rhythm, practice, calibration, reset, belonging, and return.
- Avoid vacation, holiday, getaway, escape, resort, and timeshare language.
- Speak to one person directly.

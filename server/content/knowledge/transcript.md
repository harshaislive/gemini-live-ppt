# Approved presentation language

## Opening tension
Full calendars do not make full lives. Most ambitious lives do not break from lack of drive. They break when there is nowhere protected for recovery, so the body keeps paying for a pace the mind has already normalized.

## Reframe
Thirty nights a year is not a fantasy number. It is enough to interrupt the long drift of fatigue without asking someone to step out of life and become someone new. The point is repeated contact with quiet, sleep, and a different pace of attention.

## Proof
Seven years of quiet work. Six collectives. 1,300 acres restored from degraded farmland. 250 families already inside the rhythm. Those numbers matter because they are not marketing language. They are evidence of stewardship.

## Experience
Coorg. Hyderabad. Bhopal. Mumbai. Different landscapes, same discipline. These collectives are restored ground where someone can return with their body and attention and meet something quieter than the pace they came from.

## Structure
Thirty person-nights a year for ten years. Immediate family can participate. Children under 12 do not count toward person-nights. Nights do not carry forward because accumulation is not the point. Rhythm is.

## Urgency
Waiting has a cost. Another year goes by and nothing in life has been protected from the pace that is wearing it down. The real loss is not only a missed season in the forest. It is another year unchanged.

## Action
Blyton Bungalow is there for one reason: to let the land answer before the commitment does. Experience first. Then decide with your feet, not your eyes.
